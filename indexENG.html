2nd Meeting of Perovskites Materials and Solar Cells

Objective: To bring together the scientific community dedicated to hybrid perovskite semiconductors, both from theoretical and experimental approaches in the different disciplines that cover the subject.
Covered Areas: Physics, chemistry and materials science, with a focus on basic science and technological productions.
Hybrid Perovskite Solar Cells: Promising research for economical and sustainable energy.

1st Meeting of Perovskites Materials and Solar Cells

In February 2020 we held the 1st symposium on perovskite materials and solar cells at the CNEA's Centro Atómico Constituyentes. On that occasion we had academic exchanges with Argentinean and German researchers; such as Dr. Kurt Taretto from Neuquén PROBIEN, Javier Schmidt and Federico Ventosinos from IFIS-LITORAL and Yesica Di Iorio and Mariana Berruet from INTEMA. Uli Wurfel from Fraunhofer-Institutfür Solare Energie Systeme ISE was present from Germany. The above-mentioned researchers contributed outstanding advances in their research and created links that have been maintained to this day. These links have been fundamental for the development of new topics related to the study and manufacture of perovskite solar cells, as well as for the exchange of ideas that have allowed students to consolidate themselves as researchers in training in the area.
